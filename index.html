About Us

Borno State, located in northeastern Nigeria, is known for its vast terrain that includes savannas, grasslands, and low-lying areas. With a population exceeding 5 million, it is a key region in terms of agricultural output and trade. Before recent disruptions, Borno had a developing education system, and transportation was crucial for connecting it to neighboring states. However, ongoing security challenges have destabilized many sectors. Utilities, such as electricity and water supply, have struggled, and the recent floods have further weakened infrastructure, wiping out entire communities.

Aims

Despite issues in education, transportation, and security, utilities have become the most pressing concern due to the recent floods that devastated entire communities. This project focuses on evaluating and improving the utility infrastructure, particularly electricity and water, which have been critically damaged. Waste management is also a key aspect, as flood debris has overwhelmed existing systems, threatening public health. Strengthening utility systems is crucial for rebuilding and creating resilience against future disasters in Borno State.

1. Project Overview
- Flood Risk Management: Highlight the importance of understanding flood-prone areas for better emergency response, urban planning, and infrastructure development.
- Data Visualization: Used google map to map critical infrastructure like roads, hospitals, schools, and flood-prone zones in Maiduguri.
2. Data Source
- Local Data: Collaborate with local government agencies for real-time flood data and satellite imagery to monitor weather patterns.
- API Integration: Use services like Open Weather for real-time weather data.
3. Key Features
- Interactive Maps: Incorporate clickable layers for viewing critical infrastructure and flood-affected areas.
- Flood Alerts: Show live alerts about current flood conditions, levels, and emergency shelters.
4. User Impact
- For Authorities: Provide data for disaster preparedness and policy formulation.
- For Residents: Offer flood warnings, evacuation routes, and nearby resources (e.g., shelters, hospitals).
5. Future Scope
- Sustainability Data: Expand to include drought monitoring, water conservation data, or renewable energy resource tracking.
- Education: Partner with local universities to train students on using the platform for research and development.
